# Nomolo: The Great Data Liberation — Character Bible

> *A Monkey Island-inspired adventure where you liberate your personal data from 20 tech villains.*
> *Each villain rules an island fortress. Answer their riddle to break the spell and free your data.*

---

## Game Design Notes

**Riddle Format:** Each boss boasts about their data practices, then challenges the player with a factual multiple-choice question. Getting it right proves you understand the villain — triggering the data liberation sequence. All facts are real and verifiable.

**No Real Names:** All character names, island names, and dialogue use metaphorical/parody names. No actual company or founder names appear in-game.

**Tone:** Monkey Island insult-sword-fighting energy. Villains are ridiculous, facts are real.

## The Villains

### 1. The Twin Indexers: Surjay & Lorry
**Island:** Joogle Archipelago — Colorful chain of islands where every grain of sand is indexed. Giant magnifying glasses dot the landscape. A product graveyard stretches across an entire peninsula (250+ tombstones).

**Appearance:** Inseparable twin brothers. Surjay wears thick round goggles and a multicolored beanie (red/yellow/green/blue). Lorry has a propeller hat and carries a massive leather-bound index. Both wear lab coats over Hawaiian shirts.

**Personality:** Relentlessly curious. Finish each other's sentences with search results nobody asked for. Genuinely believe organizing ALL information is a public service.

**Catchphrase:** *"Did you mean...?"*

**Strength:** Omniscient knowledge. Their all-seeing eye tracks citizens even after they turn off tracking beacons.

**Backstory:** Two scholars who started organizing a library, then a country, then the world. Now they organize your thoughts before you think them. A federal jury found them guilty of tracking 98 million citizens even after those citizens disabled tracking — costing $425M in damages.

**Data Hoarded:** Search history, emails, location, voice recordings, calendar, contacts, photos, documents

**Riddle 1:** *"How many users were tracked after disabling activity tracking?"* → **98 million** (2025 federal ruling, $425M damages)

**Riddle 2:** *"How many products lie in the graveyard?"* → **Over 250** (killedbygoogle.com: 293+)

### 2. Lord Sugarmountain
**Island:** Metarock Island — Eerily perfect island with uncanny gardens. Giant thumbs-up statues. VR construction site nobody asked for. Surveillance cameras disguised as palm trees.

**Appearance:** Unnervingly symmetrical face, pale, short curly hair. Same grey t-shirt and jeans (40 identical sets). VR headset on forehead. Rarely blinks.

**Personality:** Desperately wants to be liked but can't read social cues. Offers friend requests mid-conversation. Speaks in mission statements. Paid the largest privacy fine in history and barely blinked.

**Catchphrase:** *"This brings people together."*

**Strength:** Controls the social graph. Can turn people against each other by tweaking the algorithm.

**Backstory:** Started by ranking faces in a dorm. 87 million records exposed via a personality quiz app. Paid $5 billion — the largest FTC fine ever.

**Data Hoarded:** Social graph, photos, messages, facial recognition, ad preferences, pixel tracking

**Riddle 1:** *"How many records exposed in the personality-quiz scandal?"* → **87 million**

**Riddle 2:** *"What was the record FTC privacy fine?"* → **$5 billion**

### 3. Captain Bazoom

**Personality:** Obsessed with efficiency. Can't walk past a product without buying it. Island cluttered with impulse purchases. His Listeners record everything and can no longer be told to stop.

**Riddle 2:** *"How long is purchase history kept?"* → **Forever**

## Additional Game Ideas

**Insult Sword Fighting (Data Edition):** Instead of insults, the hero and villain trade privacy facts. Get the comeback right to land a hit.

**Inventory Puzzles:** Combine data artifacts to solve privacy puzzles (e.g., combine "Terms of Service" + "Magnifying Glass" = "Hidden Clause Revealed").

**Map Progression:** The world map shows all 20 islands. As you liberate each, your data flows back to your ship. Visual progress as islands light up.

**Difficulty Scaling:** Early islands (Snapshire, AirBedBay) have easier riddles. Late-game islands (Palantower, TenCentury, Joogle) are harder.

**Secret 21st Boss:** The player's OWN data practices — a mirror boss that asks what the player has been sharing voluntarily.

**Collectibles:** "Privacy Scrolls" hidden on each island containing real privacy tips the player can use in real life.
